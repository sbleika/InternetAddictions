Internet Addiction

What is internet addiction?

Internet addiction is when a person has a compulsive need of spending several hours on the internet,
and they just can’t control themselves.

Do I have an Internet addiction?

To know if I have internet addiction I must recognize symptoms such as agitation when I step away from my electronic device, or anger as well, fatigue, insomnia, constantly having headaches, poor concentration capabilities or academic difficulties.

What types are Internet addictions are there?

There are 5 types of internet addiction. Cybersex Addiction, Net Complusions, Cyber (Online) Relationship Addiction, Compulsive Information Seeking and Computer or Gaming Addiction.
For more information about these visit Addiction Center

What can I do if I think I have internet addiction?

Actually it’s going to depend on the severity of the addiction or behaviors, so that there would be different treatment for them. If you know someone who has internet addiction is suffering from internet abuse, the first step is to plan an intervention. Generally, therapy is incorporated into the treatment of addiction or any other disorder that can present anxiety, depression, or insomnia. In some cases, medication can be used to control intrusive thoughts about going online if other treatment options weren’t effective. The life of you or someone you care about does not need to be controlled by internet addiction. Consider conducting additional research to establish the best course of action, but keep in mind that time is an important resource and may not be best dedicated excessively to the technological tools that are intended to benefit us (and not the other way around). If you've made the decision to move forward, speak with a therapist right away to find out what therapy options are available to you.

How does internet addiction affect your life?

A person's emotional and physical health may suffer significantly as a result of an internet addiction. Physical issues brought on by an internet addiction include body aches, Carpal Tunnel Syndrome, insomnia, visual issues, and weight gain or loss. Depression, dishonesty, anxiety, social isolation, violence, and mood swings are a few examples of emotional impacts.
